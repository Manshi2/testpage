    Complimentary cultural activities at OUTRIGGER Reef Waikiki Beach Resort

    Experience what makes OUTRIGGER Reef Waikiki Beach Resort such a special place. Immerse yourself in Hawaiian culture and traditional Polynesian activities from hula dancing to ukulele lessons without leaving the resort.  

    A’o Cultural Center

    The A‘o Cultural Center serves as a vibrant hub for guests to engage in a wide range of Hawaiian cultural activities. Visitors can participate in hula lessons, learn to play Hawai‘i’s official instrument—the ‘ukulele—craft traditional lei or kukui nut kupe‘e bracelets, and explore many other hands-on experiences that celebrate the rich heritage of Hawai‘i.

    Live local music

    Enjoy nightly live Hawaiian music from award-winning musicians such as Kawika Kahiapo and Hookena on the large outdoor stage at the Kani Ka Pila Grille. You’ll find good vibes, jam sessions, and hula dancing at this open-air dining and entertainment venue by the pool.

    Native island arts and crafts

    Get a glimpse into Hawaii’s unique culture with hula lessons, lei making, and island arts and crafts. You’ll also find inspiring art and exhibits throughout the lobby, including illustrations depicting Hawaiian heritage and a 30-foot hulled canoe. Stop by our A'o Cultural Center for exhibits on sustainable coastlines and Polynesian history.

    Lei making

    Explore your artistry creating your very own traditional flower lei, stringing together the beautiful and symbolic orchid flower. 

    Hula lessons

    More than a dance, hula tells the story of Hawaii's ancestral knowledge. Immerse yourself in this beautiful art form that captures the true essence of Hawaii.

    Rhythms of the ocean

    Join us for sunrise yoga and hula with the sand beneath your feet during our morning fitness and movement class. Begin with breathing and gentle stretches to melodic Hawaiian music as our instructor guides you through upper body exercises using rhythmic motions that describe the beauty of our island.

    Sunset ceremony

    Watch the sunset and bid farewell to the day with Aloha while participating in a traditional Hawaii Sunset Ceremony.

    Kupee (bracelet) making

    Create an authentic kupee (bracelet) and take the perfect fashion adornment home with you as a reminder of your time in Hawaii.

    Ukulele lessons

    Try your hand at Hawaii's most beloved instrument. Hear happy notes spring from the ukulele as you play your first song. Every strum will make you smile.

    Waikiki Beach Walk

    Walk along this vibrant promenade on Waikiki Beach. Enjoy a lively shopping, dining, and entertainment experience with oceanfront restaurants, stores, art, galleries, and cultural activities that embrace the aloha spirit.  

    Learn more Waikiki Beach Walk

    Iolani Palace

    Built in 1882 by the last king of Hawaii, King Kalakaua, Iolani Palace was the official residence of the Hawaiian monarcy. Step back in time and into the rooms where the king and queen entertained dignataries from around the world. 

    Learn more Iolani Palace

    Pearl Harbor

    Visit the Pearl Harbor National Memorial and learn about one of the most pivotal moments in US history: the attack on Pearl Harbor. Take a tour of the legendary battleships and museums before paying respects at the Pearl Harbor Memorials.  

    Learn more Pearl Harbor

    Honolulu Zoo

    Discover 1,200 animals at this iconic zoo located on 42 acres in Kapiolani park. See amazing wildlife like tigers, giraffes and Komodo dragons.

    Learn more Honolulu Zoo

    Waikiki Aquarium

    Explore the interactive tide pool and exciting exhibits to see tropical fish, monk seals, live corals, sharks, and jellyfish living in harmony at Oahu’s best family attraction.  

    Learn more Waikiki Aquarium
